Founders' Age

Written by Ronan Mc Guire

Recently I noticed something that should have been obvious. All of the startups blogs I follow, and most of the twitter accounts I follow regularly, are are wrriten by middle-aged founders.

Despite all of the hullabulah around young founders and young 'innovators', they simply don't have many interesting things to say.

There is nothing like hearing war stories from experienced founders. The shit they went through, the happy times, and how they reacted to strugggles.

A lot can be learned from succintly-told stories, distilled from years on the field. And a lot of mistaked can be avoided.

Experience is interesting, as it turns out.

Given that the average age of a founder in Silicon Valley is 39 years old, experience might be profitable, too.
